to become confident performers and makers of art. As students work together

<h4><strong>CCA Highlights</strong></h4>
<h4>#TellStory Season 11 2026</h4>
<p>Our Speech &amp; Drama Club students proudly presented an adaptation of
the musical <em>Matilda</em> at <em>#TellStory Season 11</em>, held at the
Black Box, Gateway Theatre, in April 2026. As the only primary school performing
alongside students from secondary schools and tertiary institutions, they
rose to the occasion with confidence and poise.</p>
<p>Performing before a full public audience, our students demonstrated expressive
storytelling, strong stage presence and excellent teamwork. Their outstanding
performance reflected not only the many months of dedicated rehearsals
since January, but also our school values of Resilience and Excellence.</p>
<p>The performance was a memorable milestone for our young performers and
a celebration of their growth, confidence and passion for the performing
arts.</p>

<h4>#TellStory Season 10 2025</h4>
<p>Our Speech &amp; Drama Club students proudly represented Marsiling Primary
School at #TellStory Season 10 at the Esplanade with an engaging performance
that highlighted the importance of environmental sustainability. Through
expressive acting, they encouraged the audience to reflect on their role
in caring for the environment.</p>
<p>The performance was the culmination of hard work, dedication and teamwork.
Our students displayed confidence, creativity and resilience as they performed
on a professional stage, delivering their message with enthusiasm and conviction.</p>
<p>The experience was a valuable opportunity for our students to grow as
performers, strengthen their communication and collaboration skills, and
inspire others through the power of drama. We are immensely proud of their
effort and achievements.</p>

<h4>Speak Good English Movement</h4>
<p>With the purpose of educating Marsilians on speaking good English, MPS
Speech and Drama Club started on a storyboarding journey with members making
use of a story plot to discuss the camera angles to film from, the props
needed and the dialogues of the characters for the respective scenes in
the story. They also drew up sketches of how the scenes would look like
in their storyboard templates. After the storyboards were completed, some
members took up the roles of filming the scenes, editing and adding in
subtitles for the skit.</p>

<h4>Total Defence Day (TDD) Skit 2024</h4>
<p>Our members put up a skit for this year’s Total Defence Day which was

evolved around the 6<sup>th</sup> pillar of Total Defence – Digital Defence.
Our members had the opportunity to showcase the skills that they had learnt
during the CCA sessions and it was a great experience for them.</p>
